Elo Ratings

Elo System Overview

Initial Ratings:

Every participant starts with a base ELO rating of 1500.

Rating Calculation After Each Competition:

Determining Actual Scores (SA):

The score is calculated based on the participant's finishing position using the formula:

SA = n-r⁄n-1, where n is the number of competitors, and r is the competitor's rank.

This gives the winner (1st place) the highest score and scales down linearly to the last place.

Calculating Expected Scores (EA):

The expected score is calculated based on the participant's current ELO rating and either the ratings of their specific opponents or the average rating in the competition, using the formula:

EA = 1⁄1 + 10^((average - RA) / 400), where RA is the participant's rating, and average is the average rating of the competition.

Updating Ratings:

Participants' ratings are updated after the competition based on their actual versus expected scores. The new rating is calculated as:

RA' = RA + K Ã— (SA - EA), where K is a constant determining the maximum adjustment (we use K=30).

Adjustments for Competition Difficulty:

Ratings are adjusted more significantly when a participant with a lower rating outperforms expectations in a higher-rated competition, and vice versa.
